What this page should answer
Who receives the link, what they must complete, what staff need to review, when payment matters, and what gets shown publicly after casting is ready.

1A
Send the registration link
Decide whether BTS sends links automatically after a role is accepted, and set the deadline families will see.
Registration Due By
The deadline shown to the performer on their registration page. Leave blank for no deadline.
Send Registration Link Automatically
When on, BTS emails the registration link as soon as a performer accepts their role.
1B
When someone accepts
Choose what BTS should do automatically the moment a performer says yes.
2A
Choose the form sections
Tick the sections you want performers to complete. Youth sections can appear automatically based on the age rules.
2B
How the form behaves
Set what performers can edit, what staff can customize, and what locks after submission.
3A
Age brackets
Set the age cutoffs that define child, teen, and adult performers. These brackets drive which registration sections appear and who is allowed to sign forms on their own. Ages are calculated from date of birth, not a stored age field.
Child Performer
Performers below this age are treated as children. Guardian and pickup rules apply.
Teen / Minor Performer
Performers in this range are minors. Guardian signature rules apply but pickup requirements depend on the child bracket.
Adult Performer
Adults can sign their own forms and do not require a guardian contact unless you override that below.
Guardian Required
Performers below this age must provide a guardian contact as part of their registration.
Can Sign Own Forms
Performers at or above this age can sign agreements themselves. Below this, a guardian must co-sign.
Pickup / Drop-off Required
Performers below this age must have approved pickup adults on file before registration is complete.
3B
Age-triggered requirements
Choose what youth and adult performers must complete based on the brackets above.
3C
Define complete
Tell BTS what has to be finished before a cast member counts as registered.
Conflict Confirmation
Whether performers must tick a box confirming they have read and accept the rehearsal schedule conflicts.
Edits After Submission
What happens when a performer tries to change something after submitting. Staff approval sends you a notification before the change goes through.
Registration Due Date
The date by which all cast members must have their registration finished. Used for reminders and completion tracking.
3D
Required information
Choose what information blocks completion. Unticked items are still tracked, but they do not stop registration from being marked done.
3E
Choose agreements
Pick the documents that must be signed before registration can be complete.
3F
Explain the signing step
Add one short note before the agreement section so families know what they are signing.
Agreement Instructions
Shown to the performer just before they reach the documents. Keep it short and friendly.
3G
Show the commitment
Put the rehearsal pattern and key dates in front of performers before they submit conflicts.
First Rehearsal Date
The date of the very first rehearsal. Shown on the registration form so performers know when things kick off.
Rehearsal Schedule
A plain-language summary of the regular rehearsal pattern. Shown to performers before they submit their conflicts.
Key Dates
Important fixed dates like tech week, dress rehearsal, and performance nights.
3H
Schedule acknowledgement
Require cast members to confirm they understand the rehearsal commitment before their registration can be completed.
3I
Visibility and release
Control when the cast list goes public and what information the public can see. Internal data like contact details, medical records, and payment info is always kept private.
Public Cast List
Hidden keeps the cast list completely private. Draft preview lets you share a link before it is officially announced. Published makes it publicly visible.
Release Date
The date the cast list switches to Published automatically. Leave blank to publish manually.
Public Info
What appears on the public cast list. Choose the level of detail that fits your programme and performer privacy preferences.
3J
Internal vs public data
These settings keep sensitive information away from the public cast list. Both are on by default and should only be turned off for productions with special data-sharing requirements.
4A
Set the fee
Add a fee only if you need one. If there is no fee, set the amount to zero and hide payment from the performer.
Registration Fee
The amount each cast member owes. Set to 0 if there is no fee.
Payment Due Date
When payment must be received by. Shown to performers on their registration page.
Payment Status Rule
Track only: payment is recorded but does not block completion. Required: blocks the completed status. Hidden: no payment section appears.
Payment Instructions
These instructions appear on the registration form so performers and families know exactly how to pay and who to contact with questions.
5A
Turn messages on
Choose which registration messages send automatically. Turn off anything you want to handle yourself.
